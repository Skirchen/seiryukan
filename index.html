About Seiryukan Dojo

Our particular style is Seizankai Toyama Ryu Batto Jutsu as taught by Hataya Mitsuo sensei, 9th dan Toyama Ryu Iaido and Kaicho of the Zen Nihon Toyama Ryu Iaido Renmei. Hataya sensei has raised the standards and requirements in his dojo above all the others in Japan to ensure his students rank among the best. At the Seiryukan Dojo, we follow his standards.

Our Classes

The class is designed to teach all the fundamentals and skills of Toyama Ryu Iaido required to test for the Shodan or First Degree Black Belt level. A typical class is broken down into group and individual training. Each student is given personalized instruction according to their skill level.

Toyama Ryu Iaido class includes instruction in the following:

Kihon - The fundamentals

Basic kamae or stances, proper footwork, and correct holding of the Japanese sword.

Sword Safety - Safe handling of a razor-sharp samurai sword is the fundamental training of Toyama Ryu Iaido. At no time is a sharp sword used in actual combat with another student.

Correct drawing of the sword. Happo-giri the Eight Cuts of Toyama Ryu Iaido.

Toyama Ryu Iaido Kata

  • 3 Shoden Kata - Tameshigiri kata
  • 8 Toyama Ryu Iaido No Kata - Kata techniques against one or more attackers
  • 6 Kumitachi Kata - 2 man kata
  • 7 Gunto Soho Kata - Original Toyama Military Academy kata

Tameshigiri

Tameshigiri or test cutting is the heart of Toyama Ryu Iaido. The ability of the student to consistently perform a perfect cut on a target is the only true way to demonstrate control of the sword. After a probationary period of training, usually around 6 months, and the approval Tameshigiri is practiced in the latter part of the class.
